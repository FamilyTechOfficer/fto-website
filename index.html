Meet Kevin

25 years at Microsoft.
Now working
for your home.

I've been using computers since the 1980s. I built my first PC as a kid — not because someone showed me how, but because I wanted to understand how it worked. That curiosity never left.

For the past 25 years, I was a software quality engineer at Microsoft, helping ship several versions of Windows. I spent years making sure complex systems worked reliably for millions of users — partnering with hardware manufacturers, game publishers and developers, and Microsoft engineers to make sure games and applications continued to work when users upgraded to a new version of Windows.

That mindset — plan it right, test it thoroughly, don't leave until it works — is the same one I bring to your home.

For over 20 years, friends and family kept calling me when their tech wasn't working. I'd show up, figure it out, and fix it. Eventually it became a running joke — I started calling myself the "Family Technical Officer." The name stuck. So did the calling.

FTO is built on one simple belief: home technology should work reliably, be simple enough for anyone to use, and have someone accountable when it doesn't. Not a call center. Not a technician who disappears after install day. A real person who knows your system, knows your home, and picks up the phone.

I handle everything from the rack to the app — equipment setup, programming, and configuration. For new construction or anything requiring cable runs through walls, I coordinate with your electrician or low-voltage contractor so everything lands exactly where it needs to be.

My Philosophy

I believe your home technology should work for you — from minimal tech to full automation. Where possible, I recommend products that keep you in control: local storage instead of ongoing cloud fees, open platforms instead of vendor lock-in, and systems that keep working even if a company changes their pricing or shuts down a service. Your data stays in your home. Your system stays yours.
